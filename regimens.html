H. pylori Treatment Regimens

The first line treatment for Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) infection depends on several factors, including the patient's history of antibiotic exposure and the local prevalence of antibiotic resistance.

First-Line Therapy

Bisthmuth Quadruple Therapy

  • PPI (standard dose) twice daily
  • Bismuth subcitrate (120-300 mg) or subsalicylate (300 mg) four times daily
  • Tetracycline 500 mg four times daily
  • Metronidazole 500 mg three times daily or four times daily
  • Duration: 10-14 days

Concomitant Therapy

  • PPI (standard dose) twice daily
  • Clarithromycin 500 mg twice daily
  • Amoxicillin 1g twice daily
  • Nitroimidazole 500 mg twice daily
  • Duration: 10-14 days

Rifabutin Triple Therapy

  • Omeprazole 40 mg three times daily
  • Amoxicillin 1g three times daily
  • Rifabutin 50 mg three times daily
  • Duration: 14 days

PCAB Dual Therapy

  • Vonoprazan 20 mg twice daily
  • Amoxicillin 1 g three times daily
  • Duration: 14 days

    • Note: PCAB: potassium-competitive acid blocker

PCAB Triple Therapy

  • Vonoprazan 20 mg twice daily
  • Clarithromycin 500 mg twice daily
  • Amoxicillin 1 g three times daily
  • Duration: 14 days

This is approved for first-line treatment and independent of clarithromycin resistance testing, but it should be reserved for patientss demonstrated to have clarithromycin-sensitive strains of H.pylori.

Clarithromycine Triple Therapy

  • PPI (standard dose or double dose) twice daily
  • Clarithromycin 500 mg twice daily
  • Amoxicillin 1g twice daily or Metronidazole 500 mg three time daily
  • Duration: 14 days

This is an option for first-line therapy in regions where H. pylori clarithromycin resistance is known to be < 15% and in patients with no previous history of macrolide exposure

Alternative Therapy

Sequential Therapy

  • [PPI (standard dose) + Amoxicillin 1 g] twice daily

    • Duration: 5-7 days, then

  • [PPI, Clarithromycin 500 mg, Nitroimidazole 500 mg] twice daily

    • Duration: 5-7 days

Hybrid Therapy

  • [PPI (standard dose) + Amoxicillin 1 g] twice daily

    • Duration: 7 days, then

  • [PPI, Amoxicillin, Clarithromycin 500 mg, Nitroimidazole 500 mg] twice daily

    • Duration: 5-7 days

Levofloxacin Triple Therapy

  • PPI (standard dose) twice daily
  • Levofloxacin 500 mg once daily
  • Amoxicillin 1g twice daily
  • Duration: 10-14 days

Second-Line Therapy

Second-line therapy should not repeat the first-line regimens that have already been used and must include at least one different antibiotic.

Rescue or Third-Line Therapy

Prior to starting rescue or third-line therapy, it is important to send an ulcer biopsy specimen for culture and antimicrobial susceptibility testing. Additionally, antimicrobials that have been previously used should be avoided.
